<Ingredients>

### Ingredients
    
  - 260 g (1/2 medium) butternut squash, de-seeded and cubed (half of an average squash)
  - 175 g (1 medium) bell pepper, any colour
  - 1 small apple 
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 1/2 tbsp ghee
  - 60 ml (1/4 cup) coconut cream
  - 1 tbsp honey
  - 1/2 tsp white vinegar
  - 1/2 tsp paprika
  - 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  - 1 tsp curry powder
  - 1/8 tsp dried ground ginger
  - 1/2 tsp salt
  - 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  - 1/4 tsp garam masala

</Ingredients>

<Instructions>

### Instructions

#### Preparation
  - Peel, de-seed and dice the butternut squash.
  - De-seed and dice the bell pepper.
  - Peel, de-core and dice the apple.

#### Method

  - **Step 1**
    - Preheat a fan-assisted oven to 200 deg C (400 deg F).
  - **Step 2**
    - Mix the olive oil and butternut squash.
    - Spread the butternut squash out on a baking sheet on a baking tray.
    - Roast in the oven for 35 mins.
  - **Step 3**
    - Around 15 mins before the squash has finished cooking, melt the lard in a large frying pan on the hob at a medium heat. (I use number 6.)
    - Add the bell pepper and fry for about 5-10 mins, until soft.
  - **Step 4**
    - Add the apple and cook until soft, about 2-3 mins.
  - **Step 5**
    - The butternut squash should now be finished cooking, add it it to the frying pan along.
    - Add the garlic, curry powder, ground dried ginger, salt, cinnamon and garam masala. 
    - Mix everything together.
    - Cook for 5 mins, stirring often.
  - **Step 6**
    - Turn the hob to low.
    - Add the coconut cream, honey, white vinegar, sweet paprika and stir.
  - **Step 7**
    - Simmer for a further 15 mins, stir occasionally.
  - **Step 8**
    - Serve.

</Instructions>
