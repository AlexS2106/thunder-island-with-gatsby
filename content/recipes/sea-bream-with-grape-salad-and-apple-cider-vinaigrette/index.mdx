<Ingredients>

### Ingredients
    
  - **Cooking the sea bream**
    - 4 x medium sea breams, about 450g each
    - 2 tsp salt
    - 2 tsp black pepper
    - 2 tsp garlic powder
    - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - **For the salad**
    - 100 g (2 cups) romaine lettuce
    - 100 g (2 cups) iceberg lettuce
    - 100 g (1 cup) cucumber
    - 150 g (2x medium) tomato 
    - 150 g (1 1/2 cups) green grapes
    - 40 g (2 x small) shallots
    - 80 g (2x sticks) celery
  - **For the apple cider vinaigrette**
    - 4 tbsp olive oil
    - 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
    - 1/2 tsp salt
    - 1/2 tsp black pepper
    - 1/2 tsp garlic powder

</Ingredients>

<Instructions>

### Instructions

#### Preparation

  - Make sure the sea breams are descaled and gutted.
  - Remove the sea breams from the fridge 30 minutes before cooking.
  - Roughly shred the romaine and iceberg lettuce.
  - Top, tail and dice the cucumber.
  - Roughly dice the tomato.
  - Halve the grapes.
  - Finely chop the shallots.
  - Finely slice the celery.

#### Method

  - **Step 1**
    - Preheat a fan-assisted oven to 200 deg C or 400 deg C.
    - Line a roasting tray with baking paper.
    - Lay the sea breams out on a roasting tray.
  - **Step 2**
    - Mix the ingredients to cook the sea breams: salt, black pepper, garlic powder and olive oil in a small bowl.
    - Use a pastry brush to cover the fish with the seasoned oil, turn over and repeat.
    - Put the baking tray in the centre of the oven for 30 mins.
  - **Step 3**
    - Mix the lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, grapes, shallots and celery in a salad bowl.
  - **Step 4**
    - In a small bowl, mix the vinaigrette ingredients: olive oil, apple cider vinegar, salt, black pepper, and garlic powder.
  - **Step 5**
    - Check that the fish is cooked before removing it from the oven. A meat thermometer should read 63 deg C or 145 deg F.
  - **Step 6**
    - To serve, divide the salad equally.
    - Place one sea bream on top of each plate.
    - Pour equal amounts of the vinaigrette over each sea bream; make sure to cover the edible flesh from head to tail.
    - Serve.

</Instructions>
